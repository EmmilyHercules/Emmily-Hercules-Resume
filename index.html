I'm a first year at University of California Riverside. I major in History and hope to work in museums one day. That way I actually get hands on experience with artifacts I spent years studying. It also allows me to educate a larger crowd of people with different backgrounds. There's a misconception that history needs to be memorized when really it should just be understood to point where you can form your own opinions. People need to understand how important it is to actually learn history since it's something that is still relevant today. People refuse to learn history because they think that everything happened in the past and that it doesn't matter anymore. When in reality, history shapes the way that we live our lives today. We should be grateful for all the things that did happen in the past otherwise we wouldn't have the lifestyle that we have now.

My father took charge in 2002 and made it into a corporation. Since we are family run, we have extremely loyal customers who have came for years. We are a multipurpose store that sells general food to tobacco to lotto tickets. It took awhile to get accustomed to all the new additions we constantly add to our store.
